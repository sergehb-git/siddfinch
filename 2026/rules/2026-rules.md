# 2026 league rules (recap)

**Teams:** **11** teams, including the **Kiev Ghosts**.

**Platforms:** The draft order is set via **Yahoo** (method TBD). League play and roster hosting use **Rotowire**.

---

## Rosters

- You must roster **at least 9 pitchers**. You may roster **10 pitchers** or **one extra batter** at any position—the **NP** (non-pitcher) slot (or DH, depending on host labeling).
- You may **switch during the season** between **9 and 10 pitchers** (and the corresponding NP/batter flexibility).

**Positions:** **5 OF**, **2 C**, **1B**, **2B**, **3B**, **SS**, **MI** (2B *or* SS), **CI** (1B *or* 3B), **NP** (or DH).

**Position eligibility:** To qualify at a position, a player needs **2 games played** there in the **immediately preceding MLB season** **or** **1 appearance** there in the **current** season. *(If the league uses a different eligibility table on Rotowire, that host setting controls.)*

## Reserves

- After everyone has set **active** rosters, each team adds **4 reserves**, **one at a time**, in **draft order**, **without bidding**.
- During the season, a team may carry **up to 5 reserves**.

---

## After the draft: transactions and budget

- **Mondays** are **transaction days**. The **first** transaction day is the **Monday following the draft**.
- Each team has a **$260 transaction budget** for roster changes over the season.
- **Minimum** transaction fee is **$1**. Most moves involve **two players** (add + cut), so **figure at least $2** per typical move.

---

## Free-agent bidding (weekly)

**Eligibility:** Any player **not owned** who is on a **National League** roster may be bid on. Players **not** in the NL (AL, Mexico, Japan, etc.) are **not** bid-eligible until they are **traded or signed** to an **NL** team.

### Weekly timeline

1. **Monday–Thursday:** Place **at least a $1** bid on any free agent. A list of players receiving bids is **published end of day Thursday**, with **which teams** are bidding (not necessarily final amounts in all cases—follow league publish practice).

2. **Public raises:** You may **raise bids publicly** and are **encouraged** to do so. The team with the **highest public bid on Thursday night’s published list** is positioned to win the player **if no further bids** arrive—**at the last published price** for that thread.

3. **Friday & Saturday:** **Sealed bids** by **e-mail**. The team with the **highest published bid** from Thursday **does not** submit a sealed bid. Sealed bids: subject line includes the player’s **NAME** and **“BID”**; **amount** in the **body**.

4. **Sunday & Monday:** If you held the **highest published bid Thursday**, you are **notified which roto-teams** are bidding on your player (**not** how much), and you may submit a **final “response”** bid. Sealed bids are **opened**. **Highest bid wins.** **Tie:** team **lower in the standings** wins. You **need not** response-bid; if **no sealed bids** are received, you get the player at the **last published price**.

### Sign-and-waive

If you bid on **multiple** players but only want **one**, you may **sign-and-waive** unwanted adds at **$2** total (**$1** sign + **$1** waive).

## Waiver wire and reserves

- Any player **cut** by a roto team goes on **waivers** and may be **claimed for $1** for the rest of the season.
- **No healthy player** may be **reserved** without passing through **waivers** first.
- Any player **officially on the injured list** may be placed **directly** on reserves. They may **remain** on reserves after activation. Players **optioned to the minors** cannot be reserves **without** being placed on **waivers** first.
- **Waiver claims** may be placed **directly** onto reserves.
- **Multiple waiver claims** same period: priority is **reverse standings** (last place first). Teams that **waived** the player are **last in priority** for **one week** while the player **clears waivers**.

---

## Rule of Four

Teams in the **top four** (**prize**) spots in the standings must put **newly purchased free agents** on the **active** roster. Teams **below** that line may assign new FA adds to **reserve** or **active**.

---

## Foreign trade policy (“free pick”)

- When a player is **traded out of the NL**, the affected roto team receives a **free pick**.
- You may use a free pick on **any player available at the time of the trade**, including players **received** in that trade.
- You **cannot** use a free pick on a player who **arrives in the NL after** your trade.
- Free picks are assigned **in real time** as trades are processed. You **need not** use a free pick **immediately**; if you wait, teams **behind you** in free-pick priority may pick **ahead** of you.
